# **Dialogue of the Macedonian and the Gills**

**Characters:**  
- **Alexander** — King of the World, suspended in a prototype glass diving bell.  
- **A Large Fish** — An ancient, cold-blooded inhabitant of the Mediterranean floor.

**Setting:**  
*Thirty fathoms below the fleet at Tyre. A sunless green twilight. The King hangs in a cylinder of glass and iron, watching the silt drift in the pressure.*

---

## **I. The Inventory of the Depths**

**Alexander:**  
(Pressing his forehead against the cold interior)  
My generals think I am searching for a way to break the harbor walls. They think I am looking for the foundations of the city. But the higher I climbed the mountains of Asia, the more I felt the sky was a wall I couldn’t pierce. I came down here to see if the world had a bottom, or if it simply dissolved into a different kind of nothingness. Is there no end to the empire of the salt?

**The Fish:**  
(Suspended, its eye a flat, gold coin)  
You bring a tiny pocket of air into a place that has spent an eternity trying to crush it. You look for a “bottom” as if it were a floor in your palace. To us, there is no bottom, only a change in the weight of the water. Why have you come to stare at the mud? Do you intend to march an army of shadows across the kelp?

---

## **II. The Futility of the Map**

**Alexander:**  
I have spent my life turning “The Unknown” into “Macedonia.” I thought that by seeing the abyss, I would finally own it. But looking through this glass, I realize that my map-makers are liars. They draw monsters on the edges of the parchment to hide the fact that there is simply indifference. You don’t fear me. You don’t even know that I have overturned the thrones of the East. To be a King where no one speaks your language is to realize you are merely a ghost in a cage.

**The Fish:**  
Fear is a luxury for those who live in the sun. Down here, there is only the rhythm of the tide and the search for smaller things to swallow. Your “thrones” are less than the whale-fall that feeds the crabs. You are agitated, little bubble. You are frantic for meaning. But the sea is not a story. It is a machine of salt. It does not need a King to give it permission to drown you.

---

## **III. The Return to the Surface**

**Alexander:**  
(Watching a trail of bubbles rise toward the distant, shimmering roof)  
Up there, they are shouting my name. They are sharpening spears for the next valley. Down here, the silence is so heavy it feels like it could break the iron. I am caught between a world that expects me to be a god and a sea that treats me like a pebble. Which one is the reality?

**The Fish:**  
The reality is the pressure. It is the only thing that is true whether you are a king or a minnow. You will go back to the air, and you will tell your poets that you saw the secrets of the deep. You will lie to them to keep from weeping. You will tell them the sea is a kingdom you surveyed, rather than a void that looked through you and saw nothing worth keeping.

**Alexander:**  
(Grasping the signal rope)  
Pull me up. The light is too thin here, and the truth is too heavy. I would rather die in the dust of a battlefield I understand than remain in a silence that makes me feel like a mistake of the gods.

**The Fish:**  
(Flicking its tail, disappearing into the murk)  
Go then. The salt will wait. Everything that falls eventually belongs to us anyway. Even the gods. Even the Macedonians.
