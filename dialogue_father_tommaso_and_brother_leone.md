# **Dialogue between Father Tommaso and Brother Leone on the Eve of the Sentence**

**Characters:**  
- **Father Tommaso** — A Dominican, his mind a fortress of Aristotelian syllogisms, now showing cracks of terror.  
- **Brother Leone** — A Franciscan, whose mysticism has curdled into a calm, desolate clairvoyance.

**Setting:**  
A high marble balcony in Rome, June 1633.  
The city below is a sea of shadows.  
Between them stands a Galilean telescope, its brass tube cold and indifferent, pointed toward the Jupiterian moons.

---

## **I. The Sentence Approaches**

**Father Tommaso:**  
(Gripping the stone railing)  
They say the sentence is drafted, Leone. Tomorrow, the old man from Florence will kneel and retract. The Earth will be ordered to stand still once more by the scratch of a pen. But tell me—can a decree of the Holy Office stop the vertigo? I look at the pavement below, and for the first time in sixty years, I feel as though the stone is falling away from my feet.

**Brother Leone:**  
The Earth has always been falling, Tommaso. We simply had the arrogance to call the fall a “foundation.” Whether the Florentine recants or burns, the stars have already spoken their cold, silent language. We are no longer the center of a temple; we are a fragment of dust caught in a draft.

---

## **II. The Center and the Abyss**

**Father Tommaso:**  
But the dignity of it, Leone! If we are the center, our sins matter. Our salvation has a theater. If we are but a wandering point in an *infinito* that knows no up or down, then the Moral Law is a ghost. To be the center was a burden, yes—the weight of God’s constant gaze—but to be a “fragment” is a death sentence. Without the “Fixed Point,” the soul becomes a vapor.

**Brother Leone:**  
You cling to the *caro inganno*—the sweet deception. You believe that because the stage is smaller, the play is more meaningful. But look through that glass. See the moons of Jupiter. They circle a world that is not ours. They do not care for our Rome, our dogmas, or our “dignity.” *L’infinità* is not a glory, Tommaso; it is a desert where no echo returns.

---

## **III. The Arid Truth**

**Father Tommaso:**  
(Voice trembling)  
If the Earth moves, then the sky is empty. The “heavens” are merely “space.” This is the *nullità* I fear. If I am not at the center of God’s eye, I am nothing. I would rather the Earth were a prison at the center of the world than a free wanderer in a void that does not recognize my name.

**Brother Leone:**  
And yet, consider the *noia*—the profound boredom of the heart. Did we not feel just as miserable when we believed the Sun circled us? The human heart is a hollow that no geography can fill. Whether we sit still at the heart of the Cosmos or fly through the ether at a thousand leagues, the *infelicità* remains the same. The movement of the planets is a great noise, but it cannot drown out the silence of a soul that finds no reason for its own existence.

---

## **IV. The Beautiful Lie**

**Father Tommaso:**  
You speak as if the Truth is a liberation. It is a shroud! To know the “Arid Truth” is to lose the power to live. We are killing the “Seeming” of the world, and in its place, we find only a machine—vast, revolving, and utterly blind.

**Brother Leone:**  
(He turns from the telescope as the first grey light of dawn touches the horizon.)  
Perhaps. We are like children who have found the door to the nursery unlocked and looked out into the dark hallway of the house. We cannot go back to the toys, Tommaso. The “Beautiful Lie” has been breathed upon, and the glass is clouded forever.

---

## **V. Dawn Over Rome**

**Father Tommaso:**  
(Pointing toward the East)  
Look. The sun begins to strike the dome of St. Peter’s. It rises in its majesty, just as it did for Joshua, just as it did for the Caesars.

**Brother Leone:**  
(Watching the light)  
It *seems* to rise. It is a magnificent theater, is it not? The light is warm, the colors are gold, and the world appears solid and chosen.

**Father Tommaso:**  
(Desperately)  
Is that “seeming” not enough? Can we not live in the appearance?

**Brother Leone:**  
It is all we have left. We shall go down, we shall read the sentence, and we shall tell the world that the Earth is the heart of all things. We will live in the “Seeming” because the “Truth” is a winter that never ends. But we will know, Tommaso. In the quiet of our cells, we will feel the wind of the void as we spin through the nothingness.
